Why Houston

The host city built for visitors who eat well.

Of the 16 North American host cities, Houston gets the most group-stage action: seven matches over three weeks at NRG, which holds 72,220 and has a roof for July afternoons that the open-air stadiums don't.

The city itself is the most international in Texas — 145 languages spoken at home, the fourth-largest population of any US city, and the food scene that goes with it. Long layovers between matches are not a problem here.

Common questions

What people ask before they hand us a credit card.

What languages does your team actually speak?

English, Spanish, French, and Portuguese — natively, not via Google Translate. If you need another language, tell us at the first call and we'll line up a local interpreter for the matches that matter.

Where do the match tickets come from?

Verified resale channels with FIFA-compliant transfer paths, plus direct allocations from hospitality partners for Category 1 / hospitality requests. We do not promise lottery tickets we don't have, and we'll tell you in writing where each seat came from.

How much does it cost to work with you?

The concierge fee is a flat percentage of the package or a per-day rate, depending on what you book. Hotels and tickets pass through at cost; you see every line on one invoice. We share the number on the first call, before any work starts.

Can I customize the itinerary as we go?

Yes — that's the whole point. The first 24-hour quote is a starting draft. We refine it across two or three rounds until the trip looks like something you'd actually want to live for three weeks.

What if something goes wrong during the trip?

You call the one number on your itinerary card. Someone from the team picks up between 7am and 11pm Central, and there's a backup line for genuine emergencies overnight. Missed flights, lost passports, sick kids — we've handled all of those.
